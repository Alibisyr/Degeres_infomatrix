Urban citizens in Kazakhstan are faced with a paradox: while rural areas have an abundance of fresh, organic food, city markets are filled with dangerous counterfeit products, such as kumis made with chemical additives and alcohol or kurt made from powder instead of real milk. The fear of purchasing these fake products is justified by alarming statistics: more than 100 meat shops in Astana operate without proper veterinary supervision. Furthermore, according to the "Sapaly Onim" project, 69% of products on store shelves do not meet safety standards, resulting in severe consequences, with over 700 people being poisoned each year, 80% of them children.
Degeres solves this problem by creating a digital "Safety Bridge" that connects rural producers directly with city consumers. We use a crowd-sourced logistics model, partnering with taxi drivers to transport fresh, authentic goods in sealed containers. Upon arrival at our central Urban Hub, each batch of products undergoes mandatory lab testing to ensure they are genuine and safe.
Our platform generates a unique digital QR code for each product, allowing consumers to easily scan and view production videos and lab reports. By combining our MVP platform with an AI-powered supplier rating system, we ensure that urban families enjoy real, traditional food, while providing a fair and direct route to market for rural producers.
